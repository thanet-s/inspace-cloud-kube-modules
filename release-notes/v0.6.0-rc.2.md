## Mutation outcome hardening

RC.2 supersedes RC.1 after destructive acceptance exposed an InSpace firewall
assignment that committed but returned HTTP 500. The provider no longer treats
an HTTP response error as proof that a mutation did not commit.

The shared SDK now proves single dispatch for all 22 mutation methods and
blocks mutation redirects. Fixed bootstrap, paid NLB, NodeLB, CSI, and
Karpenter persist exact intent and issued receipts before additive,
replacement, relationship-removal, and destructive cloud mutations. No HTTP
error status, timeout, malformed response, transport failure, or controller
restart can authorize a blind second dispatch. Exact
duplicate firewall and paid-NLB relationship rows are normalized as set
semantics; malformed rows and conflicting ownership still fail closed.

After each Kubernetes or status-store CAS, every controller mutation path
repeats its exact target or create-absence authority immediately before
dispatch. The file-journal live probe does the same after fsync.
Mutation-response UUIDs remain provisional until canonical ownership readback
promotes the unique intended resource; a valid-looking foreign UUID is never a
cleanup anchor.

Bootstrap stores a bounded mutation ledger in `InSpaceCluster.status` and the
standalone controller atomically persists that status in its cluster YAML.
Fixed control-plane VM POSTs are now serialized
(`maxParallelControlPlaneCreates: 1`) so each VM's restrictive firewall
assignment is authoritatively proven before the next VM is created; subsequent
boots may overlap.
CSI stores immutable mutation receipts as namespace-scoped Kubernetes Leases.
CCM and Karpenter use exact-UID Kubernetes owners and retain finalizers while an
issued operation is unresolved. Karpenter retries unrelated NodeClaim update
conflicts without repeating cloud cleanup, but refuses to release protection if
provider-owned fence state changed after the cleanup audit.

CCM serializes every firewall relationship through its canonical owner. A
firewall relationship removal needs two durable absence observations separated
by 30 seconds before its issued receipt can clear; a transient omission
followed by reappearance cannot replay the DELETE. ICMP, shard, and Service
firewall creates also repeat deterministic-name inventory after the owner CAS,
adopting one exact match and dispatching only from exact absence. Create
response UUIDs stay provisional until that canonical readback. ICMP and shard
firewall update authority likewise uses exact fresh-object CAS, preventing
overlapping CCM leaders from issuing the same mutation twice.

Karpenter coordinates base-firewall assignments and detachments through one
fixed `karpenter-inspace-firewall-mutations` Lease containing independent
per-firewall CAS receipts. Only the CAS-winning invocation may dispatch;
restarts are read-only, unrelated firewalls remain independent, and RBAC write
access restricts patch/update to this Lease and Karpenter leader election;
namespace-wide Lease create remains necessary to initialize them. Karpenter
cleanup touches only its exact base firewall, leaving CCM-owned firewalls to
CCM. Every destructive/removal step retains its issued receipt until three
complete authoritative observations at least 30 seconds apart; a reappearance
resets the sequence, and a restart before terminal persistence starts the reads
again without replaying the cloud request. VM create repeats deterministic-name,
ownership, firewall, FIP-collision, and VPC authority after the NodeClaim CAS.
Its response UUID is only a hint until canonical detail, billing, and exact
configured-VPC membership prove it.

Karpenter upgrades existing v2 NodeClaim create fences conservatively to v3.
The v3 receipt also fences the exact base-firewall relationship and FIP PATCH;
an unresolved legacy issued operation remains read-only instead of gaining a
new mutation attempt after upgrade.

Issued receipts are safety locks, not retry timers. A still-visible resource or
relationship never authorizes another DELETE/POST, and repeated empty list
responses do not clear a receipt without the component's spaced exact-absence
proof. Operator recovery requires provider-side proof that the exact request
reached a terminal no-commit result.

Bootstrap VM deletion now corroborates every spaced absence observation across
the exact VM endpoint, the location-wide VM inventory, and configured VPC
membership. A positive or split read resets the persisted evidence. Paid NLB
and CSI mutations likewise require exact billing-account, VPC, and canonical
resource identity. CSI exact-reads the union of every unfiltered location VM
row and every configured-VPC member before treating a disk as detached, so an
attachment outside the configured VPC fails closed. Disk-delete and detach
operations complete only after three Lease-persisted absence observations at
least 30 seconds apart; reappearance clears only that evidence.

The maintainer `make live-test` firewall probe now follows the same rule with a
mode-0600 local receipt. After receipt fsync, create repeats exact
deterministic-name inventory and delete repeats exact UUID/name/account/policy
and empty-assignment authority before dispatch. The create response UUID is
diagnostic only. Delete completion requires three journal-persisted absence
observations at least 30 seconds apart; restart resumes the count and
reappearance resets it. The former direct multi-resource Go lifecycle targets
are retired because they had no process-restart coordinator. Full-cluster E2E
is the component release-acceptance path.

The live audit and immutable E2E account baseline enumerate every location
returned by the InSpace location API. Local receipt writes and removals fsync
both the file and parent directory, and black-box tests cover committed and
uncommitted HTTP 4xx/5xx responses plus connection loss for POST and DELETE.

## Acceptance status

Local unit, race, vet, Helm, image, and static acceptance gates plus full
destructive real-cloud acceptance are required before this candidate can be
promoted to stable. These notes do not claim that RC.2 has passed those gates
yet.

## Endpoint-local public Services

RC.2 retains the `public-node-local` fifth load-balancing mode introduced in
RC.1, including TCP/UDP low-port exposure, real client-address preservation,
exact edge-pool ownership checks, and forced replacement acceptance.
