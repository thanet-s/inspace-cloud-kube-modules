## Breaking upgrade note

This candidate changes immutable fixed-node cloud-init and advances the
control-plane and bastion ownership schema from RC.1 v4 to v5. Existing RC.1
fixed VMs are not adopted in place; destroy and recreate the cluster to apply
RC.2. Teardown continues to recognize supported older fixed-node records,
including the v3 records from v0.4.0 and the v4 records from RC.1.

Karpenter bootstrap advances from RC.1 v9 to v10, so workers rendered by the
previous candidate are eligible for drift replacement through the normal
NodeClaim lifecycle.

## Local hostname resolution

- Replaces any stale `127.0.1.1` entry with the exact generated guest hostname
  on the bastion, all control planes, and Karpenter workers.
- Verifies the mapping with `getent` before package installation or resolver
  replacement, keeping early cloud-init independent of DHCP-provided hostname
  resolution.

## Bootstrap cache reliability and inventory

- Retries the writable seed-registry startup and the final forced recreation
  of the read-only registry plus NGINX with bounded incremental backoff and at
  most nine Compose attempts per startup.
- Derives the seed manifest from `spec.rke2.disable`. The complete audited
  inventory remains 34 images; when `rke2-ingress-nginx` is disabled, its
  webhook-certgen and ingress-controller images are omitted, leaving the
  32-image seed used by the E2E cluster.

## Validation scope

- Unit and static contracts cover hostname mapping, fixed-node schema v5,
  Karpenter bootstrap v10, bounded Compose startup, and both 34- and 32-image
  inventory forms.
- The destructive E2E configuration disables `rke2-ingress-nginx` and expects
  the derived 32-image cache seed.
